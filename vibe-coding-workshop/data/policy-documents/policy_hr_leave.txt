HR LEAVE POLICY

1. ANNUAL LEAVE
1.1 All permanent employees are entitled to 12 days of paid annual leave per year.
1.2 Leave year runs from April 1st to March 31st.
1.3 Annual leave cannot be carried forward beyond March 31st except with special approval.
1.4 Employees must submit leave requests at least 2 weeks in advance.
1.5 Leave requests are subject to departmental approval and workload considerations.
1.6 Emergency annual leave may be granted with manager approval but requires immediate notification.

2. SICK LEAVE
2.1 Employees are entitled to 8 days of paid sick leave per year.
2.2 Sick leave for up to 3 days does not require medical documentation.
2.3 Sick leave exceeding 3 days requires a valid medical certificate.
2.4 Medical certificates must be submitted within 5 days of returning to work.
2.5 Chronic illness leave may be granted with proper medical documentation.
2.6 Unused sick leave cannot be carried forward to the next year.

3. MATERNITY LEAVE
3.1 Female employees are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave.
3.2 Maternity leave can be taken up to 8 weeks before expected delivery date.
3.3 Additional maternity leave may be granted with medical certification.
3.4 Paternity leave of 15 days is available for male employees.
3.5 Adoption leave of 12 weeks is available for adopting parents.

4. COMPASSIONATE LEAVE
4.1 Employees are entitled to 3 days of compassionate leave for immediate family bereavement.
4.2 Compassionate leave requires immediate notification to HR.
4.3 Additional compassionate leave may be granted based on circumstances.
4.4 Compassionate leave applies to spouse, children, parents, and siblings.

5. EDUCATIONAL LEAVE
5.1 Employees may request educational leave for professional development.
5.2 Educational leave requires approval from department head and HR.
5.3 Maximum educational leave per year is 5 days.
5.4 Educational leave is unpaid unless specified otherwise in employment contract.

6. LEAVE PROCEDURES
6.1 All leave requests must be submitted through the HR portal.
6.2 Leave requests must include reason, dates, and contact information.
6.3 Emergency leave requires immediate verbal notification followed by written request.
6.4 Leave balances are updated monthly and available on the HR portal.
6.5 Employees must update out-of-office messages and inform team members.

7. LEAVE APPROVAL AUTHORITY
7.1 Annual leave up to 5 days: Department Head approval
7.2 Annual leave exceeding 5 days: Department Head + HR approval
7.3 Sick leave: Self-certification for 3 days, Manager approval beyond
7.4 Maternity leave: HR approval with medical documentation
7.5 Compassionate leave: Manager approval with HR notification

8. LEAVE CARRY FORWARD AND ENCASHMENT
8.1 Annual leave carry forward limited to 5 days with HR approval.
8.2 Carry forward leave must be used within first quarter of new year.
8.3 Leave encashment available for unused annual leave at year-end.
8.4 Encashment calculated based on basic salary rate.
8.5 Maximum encashment limited to 5 days per year.

9. LEAVE DURING PROBATION
9.1 Probationary employees entitled to sick leave and compassionate leave.
9.2 Annual leave available after successful completion of probation period.
9.3 Probation period leave may affect confirmation timeline.
9.4 Special leave arrangements during probation require HR approval.

10. MISCELLANEOUS LEAVE PROVISIONS
10.1 Leave without pay may be granted for exceptional circumstances.
10.2 Leave during notice period requires management approval.
10.3 Employees on leave must be accessible for urgent matters.
10.4 Leave abuse may result in disciplinary action.
10.5 Leave policy subject to change with prior notice.
