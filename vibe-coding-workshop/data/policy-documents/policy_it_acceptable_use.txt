IT ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
1.1 This policy defines acceptable use of company IT resources and equipment.
1.2 Applies to all employees, contractors, and temporary staff.
1.3 Covers all company-owned devices, networks, and software systems.
1.4 Extends to company data accessed from personal devices.
1.5 Violations may result in disciplinary action up to termination.

2. AUTHORIZED USE
2.1 IT resources may be used for legitimate business purposes only.
2.2 Personal use permitted during breaks and non-working hours.
2.3 Personal use must not interfere with business operations.
2.4 Personal use must not consume excessive bandwidth or storage.
2.5 Personal use must not violate any laws or company policies.

3. PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES
3.1 Accessing, storing, or distributing inappropriate content.
3.2 Installing unauthorized software or applications.
3.3 Connecting unauthorized devices to company networks.
3.4 Sharing login credentials or passwords.
3.5 Bypassing security controls or firewalls.
3.6 Engaging in illegal activities using company resources.
3.7 Harassment, discrimination, or threatening communications.
3.8 Unauthorized data mining or web scraping.
3.9 Cryptocurrency mining or blockchain operations.
3.10 Peer-to-peer file sharing of copyrighted material.

4. EMAIL AND COMMUNICATION
4.1 Company email for business communication only.
4.2 Personal email use limited to non-working hours.
4.3 Email attachments must be scanned for viruses.
4.4 Forwarding company email to personal accounts prohibited.
4.5 Bulk email sending requires prior approval.
4.6 Email retention: 90 days for most communications.
4.7 Sensitive information must be encrypted in emails.

5. INTERNET USAGE
5.1 Internet access provided for business research and communication.
5.2 Social media access permitted during breaks.
5.3 Streaming video/audio limited to non-working hours.
5.4 Downloading large files requires approval.
5.5 Personal browsing must not exceed 30 minutes per day.
5.6 Proxy servers and VPNs prohibited without approval.
5.7 Online shopping and banking limited to lunch breaks.

6. SOFTWARE AND APPLICATIONS
6.1 Only company-approved software may be installed.
6.2 Software installation requires IT department approval.
6.3 Personal software installation strictly prohibited.
6.4 Open source software requires security review.
6.5 Software updates must be installed promptly.
6.6 License compliance mandatory for all software.
6.7 Evaluation software must be removed after trial period.

7. DATA SECURITY AND PROTECTION
7.1 Company data classified as Confidential, Internal, Public.
7.2 Confidential data requires encryption and access controls.
7.3 Data transfers to external storage devices prohibited.
7.4 Cloud storage requires IT security approval.
7.5 Personal cloud services prohibited for company data.
7.6 Data backup responsibilities defined by department.
7.7 Lost or stolen devices must be reported immediately.

8. DEVICE MANAGEMENT
8.1 Company devices assigned to specific employees only.
8.2 Device sharing prohibited without IT approval.
8.3 Devices must be password protected with strong passwords.
8.4 Device location services must remain enabled.
8.5 Remote wipe capability enabled on all mobile devices.
8.6 Personal devices used for work must comply with security standards.
7.7 Device return required upon employment termination.

9. NETWORK SECURITY
9.1 Wi-Fi networks secured with WPA2 or better encryption.
9.2 Public Wi-Fi prohibited for accessing company systems.
9.3 VPN required for remote access to company network.
9.4 Network monitoring and logging implemented for security.
9.5 Unauthorized network devices will be blocked.
9.6 Network segmentation implemented for different security levels.
9.7 Intrusion detection systems monitor network traffic.

10. PASSWORD AND AUTHENTICATION
10.1 Passwords must be at least 8 characters with complexity requirements.
10.2 Passwords must be changed every 90 days.
10.3 Previous passwords cannot be reused for 12 months.
10.4 Multi-factor authentication required for remote access.
10.5 Password managers recommended for secure storage.
10.6 Default passwords must be changed on first login.
10.7 Shared accounts prohibited except with IT approval.

11. REMOTE WORK POLICY
11.1 Remote work requires secure internet connection.
11.2 VPN connection mandatory for accessing company resources.
11.3 Remote work area must be secure and private.
11.4 Company devices must remain in secure location when not in use.
11.5 Remote work hours must be documented and approved.
11.6 Video conferencing etiquette must be followed.
11.7 Data protection protocols apply to remote work locations.

12. MONITORING AND COMPLIANCE
12.1 IT systems monitored for security and performance.
12.2 Email and internet usage may be monitored.
12.3 File access and modifications logged.
12.4 Security incidents must be reported immediately.
12.5 Compliance audits conducted quarterly.
12.6 Violations documented and reported to management.
12.7 Cooperation with IT security investigations required.

13. INCIDENT RESPONSE
13.1 Security incidents must be reported to IT security team.
13.2 Incident response team activated for major breaches.
13.3 Evidence preservation required for investigations.
13.4 Business continuity plans activated during major incidents.
13.5 Post-incident analysis conducted for improvement.
13.6 Legal notification requirements for data breaches.
13.7 Employee training provided for security awareness.

14. POLICY ENFORCEMENT
14.1 Violations may result in disciplinary action.
14.2 Serious violations may result in termination.
14.3 Illegal activities reported to law enforcement.
14.4 Policy violations documented in employee records.
14.5 Repeated violations result in escalated consequences.
14.6 Whistleblower protection for reporting violations.
14.7 Policy reviewed and updated annually.
