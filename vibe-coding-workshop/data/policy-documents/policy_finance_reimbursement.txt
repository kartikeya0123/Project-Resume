FINANCE REIMBURSEMENT POLICY

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
1.1 This policy governs employee expense reimbursement procedures.
1.2 Applies to all employees incurring business-related expenses.
1.3 Covers travel, entertainment, training, and miscellaneous expenses.
1.4 Requires proper documentation and approval processes.
1.5 Ensures compliance with tax and accounting regulations.

2. ELIGIBLE EXPENSES
2.1 Business travel expenses (transportation, accommodation, meals)
2.2 Client entertainment and business meals
2.3 Professional development and training costs
2.4 Office supplies and equipment purchases
2.5 Communication expenses (phone, internet when work-related)
2.6 Vehicle expenses for business use (mileage, fuel, parking)
2.7 Home office expenses for remote work arrangements
2.8 Miscellaneous business expenses with prior approval

3. INELIGIBLE EXPENSES
3.1 Personal expenses not related to business activities
3.2 Commuting costs between home and regular workplace
3.3 Lunch expenses unless meeting with clients or business travel
3.4 Alcohol and entertainment without business purpose
3.5 Fines, penalties, or traffic violations
3.6 Personal insurance premiums
3.7 Family member travel or entertainment expenses
3.8 Political contributions or donations
3.9 Gifts exceeding policy limits without approval
3.10 Expenses claimed from other sources

4. APPROVAL REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Expenses under ₹5,000: Manager approval required
4.2 Expenses ₹5,000-₹25,000: Manager + Department Head approval
4.3 Expenses exceeding ₹25,000: Manager + Department Head + Finance approval
4.4 International travel: Additional approval from Executive Director
4.5 Training expenses: HR approval required in addition to manager
4.6 Client entertainment: Client relationship manager approval
4.7 Equipment purchases: IT department approval for technology items

5. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
5.1 Original receipts required for all expenses over ₹500
5.2 Credit card statements acceptable for expenses under ₹500
3.3 Travel itineraries and boarding passes for air travel
4.4 Hotel folios showing itemized charges
5.5 Conference registration confirmations and agendas
6.6 Vehicle mileage logs with date, purpose, and distance
7.7 Client meeting details (names, purpose, business outcome)
8.8 Training certificates and course completion proof
9.9 Multiple receipts must be taped to A4 paper, not stapled
10.0 Digital receipts must be clear and show all details

6. SUBMISSION DEADLINES
6.1 Expense reports must be submitted within 30 days of incurring expense
6.2 Monthly submission deadline: 5th of each month for previous month expenses
6.3 Quarterly submission deadline: 15th of month following quarter end
6.4 Late submissions may be rejected without special approval
6.5 Expenses older than 90 days require special approval
6.6 Year-end deadline: December 15th for all expenses in current year
6.7 New employee expenses: Submit within first 30 days of employment

7. REIMBURSEMENT PROCESS
7.1 Submit expense report through finance portal
7.2 Attach all required documentation digitally
7.3 Obtain required approvals before submission
7.4 Finance review within 5 business days
7.5 Payment processing within 10 business days of approval
7.6 Reimbursement via direct deposit to employee bank account
7.7 Email notification sent when payment processed
7.8 Expense report status available on finance portal

8. TRAVEL EXPENSES
8.1 Air travel: Economy class required, business class with approval
8.2 Hotel accommodation: Mid-range hotels preferred, luxury with approval
8.3 Meal allowances: ₹1,500 per day for metro cities, ₹1,200 for other cities
8.4 Local transportation: Auto-rickshaw, taxi, or ride-sharing receipts
8.5 Mileage reimbursement: ₹8 per kilometer for personal vehicle use
8.6 Airport transfers: Economy transportation required
8.7 Tips and gratuities: Maximum 10% of service charge
8.8 Travel insurance: Required for international travel

9. ENTERTAINMENT EXPENSES
9.1 Client meals: ₹1,500 per person limit without prior approval
9.2 Group entertainment: ₹5,000 per event limit
9.3 Holiday gifts: ₹2,000 per client annual limit
9.4 Conference social events: ₹3,000 per person limit
9.5 Team building events: ₹1,000 per employee quarterly limit
9.6 Welcome/farewell parties: ₹5,000 per event limit
9.7 Alcohol expenses: 25% maximum of total entertainment bill
9.8 Entertainment requires business purpose documentation

10. TRAINING AND DEVELOPMENT
10.1 Professional courses: Up to ₹25,000 per year with approval
10.2 Conference attendance: Registration fees + reasonable expenses
10.3 Certification exams: Exam fees + study materials up to ₹15,000
10.4 Books and publications: ₹5,000 per year limit
10.5 Online courses: Up to ₹10,000 per year with manager approval
10.6 Industry membership fees: ₹10,000 per year limit
10.7 Training travel expenses follow standard travel policy
10.8 Training requires completion certificate for reimbursement

11. HOME OFFICE EXPENSES
11.1 Internet allowance: ₹1,500 per month for approved remote workers
11.2 Phone allowance: ₹1,000 per month for business use
11.3 Office supplies: ₹2,000 per quarter limit
11.4 Ergonomic equipment: Up to ₹10,000 with medical recommendation
11.5 Furniture: Company-provided preferred, reimbursement with approval
11.6 Utilities: Not reimbursable for home office
11.7 Home office expenses require formal remote work agreement
11.8 Equipment remains company property when purchased

12. VEHICLE EXPENSES
12.1 Personal vehicle mileage: ₹8 per kilometer
12.2 Fuel receipts: Required when not claiming mileage
12.3 Parking fees: Reimbursable for business-related parking
12.4 Toll charges: Reimbursable for business travel
12.5 Vehicle maintenance: Not reimbursable for personal vehicles
12.6 Rental cars: Economy class required
12.7 Fuel cards: Company fuel cards available for frequent travelers
12.8 Mileage logs: Required for all mileage claims

13. CURRENCY AND FOREIGN EXCHANGE
13.1 Foreign expenses reimbursed in INR at prevailing exchange rate
13.2 Exchange rate used: Rate on date of expense submission
13.3 Foreign transaction fees: Reimbursable up to 3% of transaction
13.4 Currency conversion receipts: Required for foreign cash expenses
13.5 International credit card fees: Reimbursable with documentation
13.6 Travel advances: Available for international travel
13.7 Advance settlement: Required within 15 days of return
13.8 Foreign tax refunds: Must be claimed and returned to company

14. SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES
14.1 Emergency expenses: Immediate notification to finance required
14.2 Natural disaster expenses: Special approval process
14.3 Medical emergencies: Travel companion expenses reimbursable
14.4 Business interruption: Additional meal and accommodation costs
14.5 Equipment failure: Rental equipment costs reimbursable
14.6 Security concerns: Additional transportation costs
14.7 Weather delays: Additional accommodation and meal costs
14.8 Policy exceptions: Written approval from Finance Director required

15. COMPLIANCE AND AUDIT
15.1 Random expense audits conducted quarterly
15.2 Tax compliance maintained for all reimbursements
15.3 GST input tax credit claimed where applicable
15.4 TDS compliance for vendor payments
15.5 Documentation retention: 7 years for audit purposes
15.6 Fraud prevention measures implemented
15.7 Whistleblower protection for reporting violations
15.8 External audit conducted annually

16. POLICY VIOLATIONS
16.1 Fraudulent claims: Immediate termination and legal action
16.2 Duplicate claims: Disciplinary action and repayment required
16.3 Incomplete documentation: Expense rejection
16.4 Late submissions: Rejection without special approval
15.5 Policy circumvention: Disciplinary action
16.6 Unauthorized expenses: Employee responsibility for payment
16.7 Repeated violations: Escalated disciplinary action
16.8 Criminal activity: Reported to law enforcement
